Sleep!
An introduction to the benefits of sleep and detrimental effects of sleep deprivation
The Benefits
The Benefits of Sleep
Sufficient and consistent sleep is a key aspect of a healthy lifestyle. Sleep is closely related to brain activity, so it follows naturally that sleep would have various beneficial effects on the brain. Beyond influencing one's mind, sleep also provides physical benefits, such as stronger hand-eye coordination and improving physical appearance.
Skill Consolidation
Sleep, which incudes amount of sleep and quality of sleep, is proven to improve consolidation of certain skills. These skills include motor(physical) skill, visual skill consolidation, declarative(fact-based) memory, and the brain's ability to process information.
Beauty Sleep
Beauty sleep is not a myth, it actually exists. Beyond feeling more energetic, sleep is also able to boost one's physical appearance. Studies have been conducted to show that those with sufficient sleep have been objectively rated as looking less tired, more healthy, and more attractive.
Creativity
Sleep creativity has been vital towards many fields, including music, technology, sports, and science. Many of these fields' leaders, such as The Beatles, Thomas Edison, and Mother Mary, have credited their discoveries and successes to sleep. More specifically, sleep is able to help one gain insight by three times as much as staying awake.
Sleep Deprivation
The Harm of Sleep Deprivation
Sleep deprivation poses an extreme danger to one's health, as it can have severe long-term and permanent effects. While one may only feel a slight lack of energy, their body suffers greatly, often without signal. Rapid weight gain, partial immune system failure, and psychological problems are some of the byproducts of sleep deprivation.
Weight Gain
Sleep deprivation causes a decrease in leptin and an increase in ghrelin. This causes the person to feel extremely hungry, which in turn increases food intake by roughly 30%. Over time, this will lead to weight gain and can even cause diabetes.
Immune System Failure
Sleep plays an important role in maintaining one's immune system. Therefore, sleep deprivation causes a decrease in one's immune system response and reduces the effectiveness of vaccines. Furthermore, due to the various other effects, sleep has been classified as a carcinogen. In extreme cases, sleep deprivation can lead to complete immune system failure and prove to be fatal.
Psychological Problems
Mood disturbances are often caused by sleep deprivation. The threat is the negative feedback loop this creates, as mood disturbances also lead to further sleep deprivation. Over time, this can cause permanent behavioral and psychological problems and even lead to development of sleeping disorders.
Remember to Sleep!
